## PART 1: ENTITY STRUCTURE RECOMMENDATION

### Option 1: Sole Proprietorship (RECOMMENDED FOR YOU)

**Definition:** You are the business. No separate legal entity. Simplest structure.

**Pros:**
- Zero registration cost (Udyam is free)
- Fastest setup (done in 1 day)
- Simple income tax filing (added to your personal ITR)
- Full control, no partner complications
- Can pivot quickly without restructuring
- Easy to transition to LLP/Pvt Ltd later

**Cons:**
- Personal liability (if sued, personal assets are at risk)
- Harder to raise institutional funding (investors want corporations)
- Cannot issue share options to employees
- All profits taxed at your personal income tax rate (0% to 42% depending on bracket)

**Tax rate:** Your personal income tax slab (likely 20-30% if you earn ₹50L+)
**Ideal for:** Solo founders, Year 1-2, revenue < ₹50L

---

### Option 2: Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)

**Definition:** Partnership firm with legal entity status. Partners have limited liability.

**Pros:**
- Legal entity protection (personal assets protected from business liability)
- Better for B2B credibility ("XYZ LLP" sounds more professional than "Vaishak")
- Can issue partnership interests to future co-founders
- Lower audit requirements if turnover < ₹1Cr
- Fixed corporate tax rate (30% on profits, no dividend tax)

**Cons:**
- Registration cost: ~₹3,000-5,000 (MCA filing)
- More compliance overhead (annual filings, partnership deed)
- Fixed 30% tax rate even if profit is small (worse than sole proprietorship at low incomes)
- Cannot claim startup tax exemption (Section 80-IAC)
- Restructuring overhead if bringing in investors

**Tax rate:** Flat 30% corporate tax + 18% GST (if applicable)
**Ideal for:** Teams with 2+ co-founders, planning to raise funding, revenue > ₹50L

---

### Option 3: Private Limited Company

**Definition:** Full corporate entity with shareholders and directors.

**Pros:**
- Full liability protection
- Can issue equity shares (for investor funding, employee ESOP)
- Professional perception
- Can conduct business globally more easily

**Cons:**
- Highest setup cost (~₹5,000-8,000)
- Highest compliance burden (quarterly GST, annual audit, MCA filings)
- Fixed 30% corporate tax
- Cannot claim startup tax exemption (Section 80-IAC) — changed in 2024, now allowed
- Overkill for solo founder with <₹50L revenue

**Tax rate:** 30% corporate tax + tax on dividends
**Ideal for:** Series A ready, multiple co-founders, multi-crore revenue

## PART 2: STEP 1 — UDYAM/MSME REGISTRATION

### What is it?

Udyam Registration is a free, government-issued classification that certifies your business as a **Micro/Small/Medium Enterprise (MSME)**. This gives you access to:
- Collateral-free loans (CGTMSE scheme)
- Priority in government tenders
- Trademark fee concessions (50% discount)
- Subsidized government schemes
- Higher tender thresholds

**Key fact:** Not registering = leaving ₹50,000+ in government benefits on the table.

### Eligibility

**Your business (Ooru Logix) qualifies because:**
- Investment in plant + machinery: Est. ~₹5-10L (well under ₹2.5Cr limit)
- Annual turnover projection: <₹10Cr (micro enterprise threshold)
- Sole proprietorship is explicitly eligible

**Classification (as of 2025-26 budget):**

| Category | Investment Limit | Turnover Limit |
|----------|------------------|----------------|
| **Micro** | < ₹2.5 Cr | < ₹10 Cr |
| Small | < ₹5 Cr | < ₹50 Cr |
| Medium | < ₹10 Cr | < ₹250 Cr |

**Your likely classification:** Micro (eligible for ₹4,500 trademark fee, subsidized loans, etc.)

### Required Documents

Gather these BEFORE you start the application:

1. **Aadhaar card** (yours, as proprietor) — mandatory for OTP verification
2. **PAN** (your personal PAN — AXXX format)
3. **GST number** (optional — you can skip if not registered yet)
4. **Bank account details** (business or personal account, with IFSC code)
5. **Business location address** (proof: Electricity bill, rent agreement, or property tax receipt for your office/workspace)
6. **NIC code** (industry classification — see below)
7. **Investment amount** (estimated capex in equipment, machinery, software)
8. **Employee count** (0 for solo founder)

### NIC Codes for Ooru Logix

**You'll need to pick ONE primary NIC code.** Select based on your main revenue stream:

- **6201** — Computer programming (primary for OLOG platform, ShopSense firmware)
- **6202** — Computer consultancy and IT consulting (web design services)

**Recommendation:** Use **6201** (Computer programming). All your revenue flows here — firmware, cloud platform, services.

## PART 3: STEP 2 — GST REGISTRATION

### When is GST Mandatory?

GST is **mandatory** only if:
- **Annual turnover > ₹40 lakh** (for goods) OR **> ₹20 lakh** (for services)
- You're selling through e-commerce platforms (Amazon, Flipkart)
- You're offering SaaS/digital services and collecting internationally

**Your situation:** OLOG + ShopSense are services/digital products. Threshold is ₹20L.

### Should You Register Voluntarily (Below Threshold)?

**YES, register voluntarily in Week 2.** Here's why:

#### Benefits of Voluntary GST:
1. **Input Tax Credit (ITC):** You can claim 18% GST paid on:
   - Equipment purchases (ESP32 boards, Raspberry Pi, sensors, etc.)
   - Software subscriptions (AWS, Firebase, GitHub)
   - Office supplies, internet, hosting
   - This offsets GST you charge customers

2. **B2B Credibility:** "GST-registered" looks professional to enterprise customers. Many corporates require vendor GSTIN before paying invoices.

3. **Zero downside if below threshold:** Even if you don't hit ₹20L, having a GSTIN costs nothing and only helps.

4. **Easy tax planning:** When you cross ₹20L (which you will), you're already set up. No rush filing when revenue spikes.

#### Cost of NOT registering:
- Missing ITC on ₹50,000 in component purchases = ₹9,000 out of pocket
- B2B deals reject you for lacking GSTIN
- Scrambling to register when you cross ₹20L

**Decision:** Register voluntarily. Takes 3-7 days, zero cost, high upside.

### HSN/SAC Codes for Ooru Logix

**GST classification uses Service Accounting Codes (SAC) for services and HSN codes for goods.**

**Your products/services map to:**

| Product | SAC/HSN | Category | GST Rate |
|---------|---------|----------|----------|
| **OLOG Platform** (SaaS) | **9983** | IT Services | 18% |
| **ShopSense (SaaS)** | **9983** | IT Services | 18% |
| **Web Design Services** | **9983** | IT Services | 18% |
| **ShopSense Hardware** (if sold) | **8517** | Communication Equipment | 18% |
| **AurumGuard Hardware** | **8543** | Electrical Machines | 18% |

**For invoices:** Use SAC 9983 for all software/service revenue. If you ever sell hardware kits, use 8517 or 8543 separately.

### Step-by-Step GST Registration

**Portal:** https://gst.gov.in

**Time required:** 15-20 minutes to file + 3-7 days for approval
**Certificate delivery:** GSTIN issued automatically in email (within 7 working days)

#### Step 1: Visit GST Portal
https://gst.gov.in → Click **"Registration"** → **"New Registration"**

#### Step 2: Enter Basic Info

- **Mobile number:** Your phone
- A temporary reference number will be generated

#### Step 3: Temporary Reference Number (TRN)
- Check your email for TRN confirmation
- Use TRN to log into GST portal

#### Step 4: Fill Registration Form (Part A: Basic Details)
- **State:** Karnataka

- **Business address:** Your Bangalore office address

#### Step 5: Upload Documents
- **Udyam Certificate:** Upload the PDF you got in Step 1 (MSME registration) — this is proof of business eligibility

#### Step 6: Submit for Verification
- System will validate Aadhaar + PAN
- New automated system (since Nov 2025) should approve within 2-3 days

#### Step 7: Receive GSTIN
- GSTIN will be emailed to you (format: 29AXXXXXXX0Z5)

- **Save this number.** You'll use it on all invoices.

**Timeline:** 3-7 working days
**Cost:** Free

## PART 4: STEP 3 — STARTUP INDIA DPIIT RECOGNITION

### What is it?

DPIIT (Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade) is a government certification for startups. It gives you:
- **3-year income tax exemption** (100% relief on profits, up to ₹100Cr turnover)
- **Patent fast-tracking** (6 months instead of 18 months for examination)
- **Patent fee waivers** (up to 80% on fees)
- **Government tender relaxations** (no experience/turnover prerequisites)
- **Labour law self-certification** (no surprise audits for 6 compliance areas)

### Eligibility

**Ooru Logix qualifies if:**
- Incorporated < 10 years ago ✓ (you're starting now)
- Annual turnover < ₹100 Cr ✓ (you're <₹1Cr)
- Works on innovation/improvement of product/process/service ✓ (ShopSense, OLOG, AurumGuard are innovative)
- Not a spin-off or reconstruction of existing business ✓ (new startup)

**Note:** You **cannot claim this as a sole proprietor**. You need to be incorporated as:
- Private Limited Company, OR
- Registered Partnership Firm, OR
- Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)

**ACTION:** For now, **skip Startup India if you're keeping sole proprietorship**. If you incorporate as LLP in Month 2, then apply.

**Alternative:** Many solo founders skip Startup India and just use Udyam + GST. The tax exemption benefit is real, but only if you're profitable in Year 1-2 (which you might not be while ramping).

### If You Want to Apply (Month 2 onwards)

**Portal:** https://startupindia.gov.in OR https://nsws.gov.in (National Single Window System)

**Process:**
1. Incorporate as Private Limited Company or LLP (₹3,000-5,000)
2. Create account on NSWS portal
3. Fill "Registration as a Startup" form
4. Upload: Incorporation certificate, PAN, business description
5. System auto-approves within 2-7 working days

**Cost:** Free
**Timeline:** 7 working days after incorporation

## PART 5: STEP 4 — TRADEMARK REGISTRATION

### Why Trademark "Ooru Logix" and "ShopSense"?

You're building brand value. Once you have customers, trademark prevents someone else from registering domain/social media/product names later.

**Cost now:** ₹4,500 per brand = ₹9,000 total
**Cost later (if disputes):** ₹2,00,000+ in legal fees to fight infringement

### Classes & Cost

**Trademark Classes:** You pick which industries your brand operates in.

**For Ooru Logix:**
- **Class 42:** IT Services, Software Development, SaaS, Web Design
- **Cost:** ₹4,500 (50% MSME discount) instead of ₹9,000

**For ShopSense:**
- **Class 9:** Software, Computer Programs, Hardware (Raspberry Pi images, sensors)
- **Class 42:** IT Services, Retail Analytics Software
- **Cost:** ₹4,500 each class = ₹9,000 total (or ₹4,500 if you only pick Class 9)

**RECOMMENDED:**
- File "Ooru Logix" in Class 42 (₹4,500)
- File "ShopSense" in Class 9 (₹4,500)
- **Total:** ₹9,000

### Eligibility for 50% MSME Discount

You get 50% off if:
1. Registered as MSME (Udyam) ✓ You'll have this by Week 2
2. Name on trademark application matches Udyam certificate

### Step-by-Step Trademark Filing

**Portal:** https://ipindia.gov.in (IP India — Intellectual Property)

**Time required:** 30 minutes to file + 18-24 months for registration
**Cost:** ₹4,500 per class (with MSME discount)

#### Step 1: Pre-Registration
- Go to https://ipindia.gov.in → **"Trademark"** → **"File Trademark Application"**
- Create account (email + password)
- Verify email via OTP

#### Step 2: Fill TM-A Form (Trademark Application)
- **Applicant details:** Your name (match Udyam certificate exactly)
- **Business name:** Ooru Logix (for "Ooru Logix" trademark) OR your name (for "ShopSense" trademark)
- **Goods/Services description:**
  - For Class 42: "Software development; IT consulting; SaaS platform development; web design"
  - For Class 9: "Computer software; software for retail analytics; computer programs"

#### Step 3: Upload Documents
- **Udyam Certificate:** Proof of MSME status (for 50% discount)
- **Logo/Brand image** (if you have one): PNG/JPG of brand name or logo
- **PAN:** Your PAN card

#### Step 4: Select Classes
- **For "Ooru Logix":** Class 42 only (IT Services)
- **For "ShopSense":** Class 9 (Software) OR Class 9 + 42 (if you want to protect services too)

#### Step 5: Apply & Pay
- Review application
- Pay **₹4,500 per class** online (debit/credit card)
- Receipt and acknowledgement will be emailed

#### Step 6: Follow-up
- Examiner reviews in ~3-6 months
- May ask for clarifications (common)
- Once approved, trademark is registered for 10 years
- Renew before expiry (in year 10) for another 10 years

**Timeline:**
- Filing to approval: 18-24 months (normal path)
- Opposition period: 4-6 months (others can challenge)
- Final registration: 24-30 months from filing

**Cost:** ₹9,000 total (₹4,500 × 2 classes)

## PART 10: COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

### ✗ Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Registering GST before Udyam:** Do Udyam first (gets you 50% trademark discount, validates business). GST relies on Udyam.

2. **Using wrong GST classification:** Use SAC 9983 (IT Services) for OLOG/ShopSense to keep it simple. Don't split across hardware/software codes unless you actually sell physical products separately.

3. **Not claiming Udyam discount on trademark:** Always mention your Udyam certificate in trademark application. Saves ₹4,500.

4. **Waiting too long to trademark:** File within 3 months of launch. Once you have customers, it's harder to prove you created the brand.

5. **Skipping GST below ₹20L threshold:** Big mistake. Voluntary GST means ITC on purchases. For a hardware+software startup, that's easily ₹50,000+ in Year 1 (esp. if you buy Raspberry Pi kits, sensors, AWS).

6. **Not opening a business bank account:** Bank transactions for business should be separate from personal. Easier to track, easier to file taxes, better for audit trails.

7. **Registering as Pvt Ltd on day 1:** Overkill. Start as sole proprietor, scale to LLP/Pvt Ltd in Year 2-3.

8. **Forgetting to update Udyam when business changes:** If you hire employees, add new products, or move office, update Udyam within 30 days. Free to update.

## FAQ

### Q: Can I start selling without registering?
**A:** Yes, legally. But you'll miss out on:
- GST input credits (lose 18% of component costs)
- Government scheme eligibility (subsidized loans, tenders)
- B2B credibility (no GSTIN = many corporates won't buy from you)
- Trademark protection

### Q: Will GST cost me more in taxes?
**A:** No. You collect 18% GST from customers, pay 18% GST on purchases, net out. ITC (input tax credit) means you only pay GST on your profit margin, not on gross revenue.

### Q: Do I need to incorporate as an LLP right now?
**A:** No. Stay as sole proprietor for Year 1. Easier, faster, cheaper. Incorporate in Month 12 if revenue > ₹25L or if you hire a co-founder.

### Q: Will Udyam + GST take a long time?
**A:** No. Udyam is same-day. GST is 3-7 days. Total process: 2 weeks.

### Q: Can I trademark later?
**A:** Yes, but you lose priority rights. Someone else could trademark your brand while you're selling. File within 3 months of launch.

### Q: How often do I need to renew registrations?
**A:**
- Udyam: Annual (free, updated during ITR filing)
- GST: Monthly filings, no renewal
- Trademark: 10-year protection, renew in year 10
- Business license: Depends on municipal corporation (usually annual, ₹500-2,000)

### Q: What's the actual cost if I do everything?
**A:** ₹9,000 (trademark only; Udyam, GST, Startup India are free). Optional: ₹3,000-5,000 if you incorporate as LLP.
