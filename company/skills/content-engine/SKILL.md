# Content Engine Skill

## Overview
Manages Ooru Logix's cross-channel content creation, calendar planning, and distribution. Focuses on authentic storytelling for Bangalore SMB retailers discovering edge AI through practical, problem-solving content.

---

## Content Calendar Framework

### Weekly Cadence
- **2 LinkedIn posts** (Tue, Thu): Founder insights, tech explainers, customer wins, industry data
- **2 Instagram posts** (Mon, Wed): Product demos, behind-the-scenes, local Bangalore shop stories
- **1 blog article** (Wed): Deep dives on SEO targets, ~2000 words, 5-7 days lead time
- **3 WhatsApp broadcast messages** (Mon/Wed/Fri): Product updates, case studies, direct retail problems solved

### Monthly Content Mix
- 40% customer/ROI focused (real shop stories, before/after)
- 30% educational (how edge AI works, retail automation benefits)
- 20% brand/founder story (building in Bangalore, team culture)
- 10% promotional (product features, limited offers, partnerships)

## Content Templates by Channel

### LinkedIn
**Brand Voice**: Direct, founder-to-founder, practical wisdom, "I learned this building ShopSense"
**Tone**: Professional but conversational, avoid jargon, lead with insight
**Structure**:
- Hook (question, stat, or bold statement)
- 3-5 line body (concrete example from a Bangalore shop)
- CTA (engagement question, link to blog/demo, or reply invite)
- Hashtags: #EdgeAI #RetailTech #Bangalore #ShopSense #AI #SmallBusiness

### Instagram
**Brand Voice**: Visual, energetic, authentic. Show the product working in real shops
**Tone**: Friendly, relatable, "this just worked for a chai shop in JP Nagar"
**Structure**:
- Hook caption (relatable retail problem or wow moment)
- Story-driven body (2-3 short sentences)
- CTA (swipe up/link, DM for demo, comment to learn more)
- Hashtags: Mix of 15-20 community (retail, Bangalore, AI) + branded (#ShopSense)

### WhatsApp Broadcast
**Brand Voice**: Warm, direct, one friend helping another
**Tone**: Short-form, scannable, mobile-first
**Structure**:
- Greeting + headline (no corporate fluff)
- 2-3 short paragraphs max
- 1 clear action (reply DEMO, visit link, learn more)
- Signature: "Team Ooru Logix 🛒"

### Blog
**Brand Voice**: Educational leader, Bangalore insider, practical teacher
**Tone**: In-depth but accessible, show your work, include real data
**Structure**:
- H1 title (target keyword in first 3 words)
- Meta description (155 chars, include keyword)
- Intro (hook with statistic or pain point, explain value in 150 words)
- H2 sections (3-5 main points)
- Real example or case study section
- CTA section (link to demo, related blog posts, product page)
- Internal links (3-5 to other blog posts or product pages)

## Content Approval Workflow

### Stage 1: Draft (Author)
- Create in shared Google Doc or Notion
- Follow template guidelines
- Tag #brand-review for review request

### Stage 2: Brand Review (2-3 hours)
- Check voice consistency (use brand-guardian skill)
- Verify accuracy (product claims, metrics)
- Ensure visual guidelines for images (if applicable)
- Return with comments or "APPROVED"
- If rejected: specific feedback + resubmit

### Stage 3: Schedule (Content Lead)
- Confirm platform-specific timing
- Schedule via native tools (LinkedIn, Instagram, Meta Business Suite, WhatsApp Business)
- Add to calendar tracking sheet (link in Slack)

### Stage 4: Publish
- Monitor first hour for comments/engagement
- Respond to comments within 4 hours on LinkedIn/Instagram
- Track initial metrics (impressions, engagement)

## Content Repurposing Matrix

**1 Blog Post → 3 LinkedIn Posts → 5 Instagram → 2 WhatsApp**

#### LinkedIn (3 posts from 1 blog)
1. **Teaser** (Tue): "Built an offline-first POS after watching a chai shop lose a day of sales to a power cut..." (link to blog)

## Image & Visual Guidelines by Platform

### LinkedIn
- **Format**: 1200x627px (landscape) or 1080x1350px (portrait, newer format)
- **Aesthetics**: Product in real shop context, founder photo, data visualization
- **Color**: Navy or chai brown callout boxes, high contrast text
- **Typography**: Inter or Lato, min 24pt readable from mobile
- **Avoid**: Stock photos (use real Bangalore shops, team photos, product in action)
- **File format**: JPEG or PNG (optimize <200KB)

### Instagram
- **Format**: 1080x1350px (feed post), 1080x1920px (Stories), 1920x1080px (Reels)
- **Aesthetics**: Bright, energetic, show product working. Bangalore street/shop context
- **Color palette**: Navy accent on chai brown background, or bright green (#2DB76F) on white
- **Typography**: Bold sans-serif (Inter), readable at mobile size
- **Avoid**: Blurry phone photos, corporate stock images, cluttered layouts
- **Real content**: Founder demo videos, customer shops, team candids, product shots at retailers
- **File format**: JPEG or PNG (optimize for Instagram compression, <4MB)

### WhatsApp
- **Format**: Adapt for mobile (all messages 1:1 phone ratio)
- **Images**: Optional, use sparingly (1 per broadcast max)
- **Layout**: Single column, short paragraphs, max 160 chars/line
- **Emojis**: 1-2 per message (chai, shop, rocket, green checkmark)
- **No images**: Bulk text is acceptable and fast-loading
- **Links**: Use short URL or built-in link preview

### Blog Featured Images
- **Format**: 1200x630px (social sharing preview size)
- **Aesthetics**: Hook + insight visual. Real shop photo OR designed image with data viz
- **Color**: Navy/chai brown header bar with white sans-serif title + green accent
- **Readability**: Legible at 300px width (thumbnail)
- **File format**: JPEG optimized <150KB
- **SEO**: Alt text = target keyword + brief description

## Crisis & Sensitive Topics

### Competitive Mentions
- Never attack competitors by name
- Focus on solutions, not critic criticism
- Example: "Most POS systems require internet. ShopSense works offline." ✓

### Technical Failures
- If product fails, acknowledge + communicate fix immediately
- Tone: "We're on it. Here's why, here's the timeline."
- Transparency builds trust > silence

### Sensitive News
- Any major retail news (job cuts, inflation, new policy) → brief to marketing team before posting
- Hold polarizing takes (politics, religion)
- Stick to practical, retail-focused insights
