# Brand Guardian Skill

## Overview
Enforces Ooru Logix brand consistency across all channels. Ensures every customer touchpoint—from LinkedIn to a shop owner's hands—reflects our voice: direct, practical, warm, and authentic.

---

## Brand Voice Rules

### Core Tone
**Direct**: No fluff, no corporate jargon, no marketing-speak.
- ✓ "We built this because power cuts destroy your evening sales."
- ✗ "Our innovative edge AI solutions leverage distributed computing paradigms."

**Practical**: Lead with the problem and the concrete fix. Speak like a knowledgeable friend.
- ✓ "This shop owner lost ₹1000 the last time the internet went down."
- ✗ "Contemporary retail environments demand resilient infrastructure."

**Warm**: Acknowledge the struggle. Show you're building with them, not selling to them.
- ✓ "We know Sunday nights are stressful. You're counting cash while worried about tomorrow."
- ✗ "Optimize your end-of-day reconciliation processes."

**Authentic**: Use real numbers, real shop names (with permission), real problems. No flowery storytelling.
- ✓ "Hari's tea shop in Rajajinagar went from 2-hour inventory counts to 8 minutes."
- ✗ "In a bustling marketplace of retail innovation..."

### Vocabulary (Use These Words & Phrases)

**Our words** (use often):
- "Edge AI" (not "distributed machine learning" or "on-device intelligence")
- "Shop owner" (not "retailer" or "merchant" or "customer")
- "Offline-first" (not "offline-capable" or "works offline")
- "Real-time" (not "instantaneous" or "near-real-time")
- "Margin" (not "profitability" or "profit optimization")
- "Billing" (not "POS transaction processing")
- "Works" (not "enables" or "facilitates")
- "Simple" (not "intuitive" or "user-friendly")
- "Bangalore retail" (not "the Bangalore market" or "metropolitan retail")
- "Bootstrap" (not "self-funded" or "capital-efficient")
- "Shop" (not "retail store" or "commercial entity")
- "Chai shop" (not "QSR" or "quick service restaurant")
- "Kirana" (not "general store" or "convenience retail")
- "We" (not "the team" or "Ooru Logix")

**Forbidden words/phrases**:
- ✗ "Disrupt" or "disruption" (overused, sounds arrogant)
- ✗ "Paradigm shift" (corporate nonsense)
- ✗ "Cutting-edge" (we're practical, not flashy)
- ✗ "Synergy" or "alignment" (jargon)
- ✗ "Leverage" (corporate speak)
- ✗ "Solutions" (vague, corporate)
- ✗ "Maximize" or "optimize" (robotic)
- ✗ "Ecosystem" (unless talking about Bangalore retail specifically)
- ✗ "Seamless" (overused, means nothing)
- ✗ "Deep dive" (cliché)
- ✗ "Value-add" (jargon)
- ✗ "Stakeholder" (use "shop owner" or "team member")
- ✗ "ROI" without context (explain what it means: margin improvement, time saved, etc.)
- ✗ "Game-changing" or "game changer" (hype, not substance)
- ✗ "Revolutionize" (arrogant for a 50-shop startup)
- ✗ Exclamation marks (except in very rare, earned moments)

### Voice by Emotion/Context

**When celebrating a win**:
- Tone: Understated, no hype
- ✓ "50 shops now. We're proud of this."
- ✗ "We're thrilled to announce explosive growth!"

**When acknowledging a problem**:
- Tone: Direct, solution-focused
- ✓ "We shipped a bug last week. Here's what broke, here's how we fixed it, here's the timeline."
- ✗ "We regret to inform you of a minor issue."

**When talking about competitors**:
- Tone: Respectful, not mean
- ✓ "Most POS systems go offline when the internet does. We built the opposite."
- ✗ "Competitor X is garbage because they don't understand India."

**When speaking to a shop owner**:
- Tone: Friend-to-friend, no BS
- ✓ "Real talk: this won't solve all your problems. But it'll solve the ones keeping you up at night."
- ✗ "Our comprehensive solution suite addresses all retail pain points."

## Brand Visual Guidelines

### Colors (RGB + Hex)
- **Navy**: #0B2648 (RGB: 11, 38, 72) — Primary, trust, professionalism
- **Chai Brown**: #8B6F47 (RGB: 139, 111, 71) — Secondary, warmth, authenticity
- **Green**: #2DB76F (RGB: 45, 183, 111) — Accent, action, growth, success
- **White**: #FFFFFF — Primary background, readability
- **Neutral Gray**: #F5F5F5 (light backgrounds), #4A4A4A (text)

### Color Usage by Context

**LinkedIn posts**:
- Navy text on white background
- Chai brown for quotes/highlights
- Green for CTAs or success metrics
- No more than 2 colors per post (plus white)

**Instagram posts**:
- Navy + green combinations for energy
- Chai brown for warmth/heritage
- White for readability
- Bold contrast (not pastels)

**Blog posts**:
- Navy H1, navy headers
- Chai brown for sidebars/callouts
- Green for success metrics and CTAs
- Warm photography (real shops, not sterile stock)

**Product/Website**:
- Navy as primary background
- White text on navy for readability
- Green for buttons/actions
- Chai brown for secondary navigation or highlights

### Typography

**Fonts**:
- **Headings (H1-H3)**: Inter SemiBold (weight 600) or Lato Bold (weight 700)
- **Body text**: Inter Regular (weight 400) or Lato Regular (weight 400)
- **Code/technical**: JetBrains Mono (monospace, for technical content)
- **Fallback**: System fonts (Segoe UI, Helvetica, sans-serif) if web fonts fail

**Font sizes**:
- H1: 32-40px (blog), 24-28px (social)
- H2: 24-28px
- H3: 18-20px
- Body: 16px (desktop), 14px (mobile)
- CTA buttons: 16px, bold

**Line height**: 1.5 for body text (readability), 1.2 for headings

### Logo Usage
- **Primary**: Ooru Logix logo with full name (navy + green)
- **Minimum size**: 120px width (don't shrink below this)
- **Clear space**: Empty space around logo = at least 10px on all sides
- **Color variants**: Navy on white, white on navy, green on white
- **Avoid**: Rotating the logo, changing colors, stretching

### Photography Style

**Real > Stock**:
- Prioritize real photos from Bangalore shops
- Real shop owners using ShopSense
- Real team members in real offices
- If using stock, choose diverse, authentic scenarios

**Aesthetic**:
- Warm lighting (not harsh/sterile)
- Show the shop in context (owner, counters, products visible)
- Genuine expressions (smiles OK, but not forced)
- Local Bangalore landmarks OK as background context

**Avoid**:
- Stock photos of "diverse team celebrating" (generic)
- Sterile product shots on white background (OK for product spec sheets, not for blog/social)
- Overly bright, "corporate" filters
- Posed, unnatural moments

### Image Optimization
- **Format**: JPEG for photos, PNG for graphics (transparency)
- **Size**: <200KB for web (use compression tools)
- **Alt text**: Always descriptive + keyword-relevant
  - ✓ "Shop owner using ShopSense billing system during afternoon rush"
  - ✗ "image123.jpg" or just "ShopSense"

## Content Review Checklist

Use this before publishing anything.

### Brand Voice Check
- [ ] No forbidden words (check list above)
- [ ] Tone matches context (direct for features, warm for customer stories, practical for tutorials)
- [ ] No exclamation marks unless genuinely earned (rare)
- [ ] Specific, concrete examples (not vague generalizations)
- [ ] Language a shop owner would use (test: would Hari from JP Nagar understand every sentence?)
- [ ] One main idea per paragraph/post (no complexity for complexity's sake)
- [ ] Removes marketing-speak (count instances of "innovative", "cutting-edge", "disrupt"—should be zero)

### Visual Check
- [ ] Colors are navy/chai brown/green + white (no other colors)
- [ ] Text is readable at mobile size (min 14px)
- [ ] Images are real, not generic stock (or justified exception noted)
- [ ] Featured image is 1200x630px (blog) or platform-native size (social)
- [ ] Alt text is descriptive + keyword-relevant
- [ ] Logo is minimum 120px if visible
- [ ] No extreme filters or saturation adjustments

### Accuracy Check
- [ ] Product claims are accurate (demo if needed)
- [ ] Metrics/stats have a source (or "based on our research")
- [ ] Shop owner names have permission to use publicly
- [ ] No false comparisons to competitors
- [ ] Links are working (test all internal + external links)
- [ ] Email addresses/phone numbers are correct

### CTA Check
- [ ] One clear, primary CTA (not 3 competing CTAs)
- [ ] CTA is specific (not "learn more", use "see demo" or "sign up for trial")
- [ ] CTA link works and goes to the right place
- [ ] CTA button/link is visually distinct (green or navy, not ambiguous)
- [ ] Mobile-friendly (tap target is at least 44x44px)

### Channel-Specific Checks

**LinkedIn**:
- [ ] Post is 100-250 words (sweet spot for engagement)
- [ ] Opens with a hook (question or stat)
- [ ] Ends with a question or CTA
- [ ] No more than 2 line breaks (LinkedIn collapses long posts)
- [ ] Hashtags are 5-8, relevant (#EdgeAI, #RetailTech, #Bangalore, etc.)
- [ ] If image, it's a real photo (not design, not screenshot)

**Instagram**:
- [ ] Caption is 125-200 words (not too long, not too short)
- [ ] First line hooks (it shows in preview)
- [ ] Has 1-2 emojis (not overstuffed)
- [ ] Hashtags are 15-20 (community + 3-5 branded)
- [ ] Image is 1080x1350px or story format
- [ ] Visual is bold/high-contrast (readable in small thumbnail)

**Blog**:
- [ ] Title includes target keyword naturally
- [ ] Meta description is exactly 155 characters (test in Google preview)
- [ ] First paragraph is punchy (under 100 words)
- [ ] Has 2-3 real examples or case studies
- [ ] Includes 3-5 internal links
- [ ] CTA is after main content, before comments
- [ ] URL is short and keyword-relevant (hyphenated)
- [ ] Featured image is optimized <150KB

**WhatsApp**:
- [ ] Message is scannable (short paragraphs, max 3-4)
- [ ] Has 1 clear CTA (not overwhelming)
- [ ] Emoji usage is minimal (1-2 max)
- [ ] Phone-friendly (no wide text blocks)
- [ ] Link is shortened or shows full URL (no cryptic shortened URLs)
- [ ] Personal sign-off ("Team Ooru Logix 🛒" or founder name)

## Channel-Specific Voice Adaptations

### LinkedIn: Professional But Not Corporate
- More formal than Instagram, less formal than a white paper
- Can reference industry trends, data, research
- Longer paragraphs OK (people read on desktop)
- Founder voice is asset (sign posts as founder when building in public)
- Numbers and metrics welcome (give context, not just stats)
- Hashtags are expected and necessary
- Be opinionated (LinkedIn rewards perspective)

### Instagram: Visual + Conversational
- One sentence per paragraph (mobile-friendly)
- Use emojis sparingly but intentionally (chai cup, shop, green checkmark)
- Casual language OK ("this is wild", "we love this")
- First line is make-or-break (appears before "more")
- Hashtags are essential (15-20 relevant)
- Calls-to-action are direct ("DM DEMO", "Link in bio")
- Relatable humor welcome (retail pain point memes)

### WhatsApp: Warm + Urgent (Without Pressure)
- Tone is friend-checking-in ("Hey! Thought you'd like this")
- No corporate language (spell it out: "shop", not "retail location")
- Personal touch (can use founder name or "Roshan here from Ooru Logix")
- Messages are brief (people are busy)
- One link per message (not overwhelming)
- CTA is gentle ("reply DEMO if interested", not "BOOK NOW")
- Timing matters (send when owners are available, not at 3 AM)

### Blog: Educational + Authoritative
- Write like you're explaining to a smart friend (not a PhD student)
- Can go deeper on technical details than social media
- Real examples are crucial (name shops/owners when possible)
- Headers are scannable (people skim)
- Data/metrics are welcome with context
- Conclude with actionable next steps
- Link generously (to other posts, product pages, tools)
- Tone is confident but not arrogant

## Competitor Mention Policy

### When to Mention Competitors
- Only when it clarifies a unique advantage
- Use initials or "most POS systems" instead of naming (less adversarial)
- Be respectful (they're solving real problems too)

### How to Mention Competitors
- ✓ "Most cloud-first systems require internet. Ours doesn't."
- ✓ "Traditional POS software wasn't built for India's connectivity challenges."
- ✗ "Competitor X is garbage."
- ✗ "All other solutions fail compared to ours."
- ✗ "We're 10x better" (arrogant, unsubstantiated)

### When NOT to Mention Competitors
- If it sounds defensive
- If it's not directly relevant to the point
- If it distracts from your own story
- If it's emotional rather than factual
- When you're insecure about something

## Crisis Communication Templates

### Product Issue / Bug
**Timeline**: Publish within 2 hours of discovering public-facing issue.

**Template**:
"We shipped a bug with [feature] on [date]. Here's what happened, who it affected, and how we fixed it.

[Specific impact statement]

[Timeline of fix]

[How we prevent this next time]

Thank you for bearing with us. We're committed to reliability first."

**Tone**: Direct, responsible, solution-focused. No excuses, no corporate apologies.

### Outage / Service Down
**Timeline**: 30-minute update, then hourly until resolved.

**Template** (initial):
"ShopSense is down as of [time]. We're investigating. Impact: [concrete description]. We'll update every hour. [Support contact]"

**Template** (update):
"Still working on it. Here's what we found [technical detail]. ETA: [specific time]. Thanks for patience. [Support contact]"

**Template** (resolved):
"Fixed as of [time]. Root cause: [what broke]. We're implementing [change] to prevent recurrence. Sorry for the downtime."

**Tone**: Transparent, specific, accountable.

### Pricing Change
**Timeline**: Announce 30 days before change (if increase), same-day (if decrease).

**Template**:
"[Date], we're changing ShopSense pricing. Here's why [specific reason], here's the new price [clear], here's how current customers are affected [specific]."

For increases: "If you bought before [date], your price stays [old price] for [duration]."

### Negative Feedback / Customer Criticism
**Timeline**: Respond within 24 hours.

**Template** (public social media):
"I hear you. [Acknowledge the specific issue]. Here's what we're doing about it. [Specific action]. Open to chat more: [DM/email]."

**Never**:
- Defend defensively
- Blame the customer
- Make excuses
- Delete the feedback
- Ignore publicly

## Brand Consistency Scoring Rubric

Use this to evaluate content before publishing.

### Score: 10/10 (Perfect)
- Voice is indistinguishable from brand guidelines
- No forbidden words, no jargon
- Concrete examples from real shops
- Warm, direct, practical
- Visual design follows color/typography rules exactly
- All links work
- Mobile-friendly and accessible

### Score: 8-9/10 (Excellent, Minor Tweaks)
- Voice is strong but has 1-2 minor adjustments needed
- One forbidden word or jargon term easily fixed
- Examples are good but could be more specific
- Visual is mostly on-brand (maybe needs image swap)
- All technical specs correct

### Score: 6-7/10 (Good, Needs Work)
- Voice is mostly there but sounds corporate in 2-3 places
- Has 3-4 words/phrases that need replacing
- Examples are generic, not specific
- Visual design is off-brand in one area (color, typography)
- Some links broken or CTAs unclear

### Score: 4-5/10 (Problematic, Major Revisions Needed)
- Voice is corporate/jargony, sounds like a typical startup
- Multiple forbidden words, unclear speaking style
- No concrete examples, all generalizations
- Visual design doesn't follow brand guidelines
- Technical errors (broken links, wrong CTAs)
- **Recommendation**: Send back for major revision

### Score: <4/10 (Reject, Start Over)
- Voice is completely off-brand
- Sounds like corporate/VC/consultant wrote it
- No real examples, all fluff
- Visual design is generic/template
- Multiple technical errors
- **Recommendation**: Reject and have author restart

## Review Workflow

### For Authored Content
1. Author drafts in Google Doc, adds #brand-review tag
2. Brand reviewer uses checklist above + scoring rubric
3. Comments with specific feedback (not vague)
4. Author makes revisions
5. If score >8/10: APPROVED, ready to schedule
6. If score 6-7/10: CONDITIONAL, send back with list of fixes
7. If score <6/10: REJECTED, request major revision

### Feedback Template
"Great start. Few things:

1. **Voice**: Paragraph 3 sounds corporate. Change 'leverage our edge AI solutions' to 'we built this to work offline.'
2. **Example**: Your metric about inventory accuracy is generic. Can you use a real shop owner's numbers?
3. **Visual**: The photo is stock imagery. Can we use a real shop instead?

Score: 7/10. Send back when fixed, and I'll re-review."

### Approval Stamp
"APPROVED FOR PUBLISHING. Ready to schedule across channels. Internal links checked. Meta description optimized. Visual guidelines met."

## Quick Reference: Brand Voice Examples

| Situation | Wrong | Right |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Describing the product | "Our innovative distributed AI platform leverages edge computing" | "ShopSense works offline. That's it." |
| Talking about a customer | "A retail merchant in our key demographic" | "Hari's tea shop in Rajajinagar" |
| Competitive advantage | "We're 10x better at optimization" | "Our system works when the internet doesn't" |
| Explaining ROI | "Maximize operational efficiency metrics" | "You save 8 hours a week, gain ₹8000/month margin" |
| Celebrating a milestone | "We're thrilled to announce exponential growth!" | "50 shops now. We're proud." |
| Addressing a problem | "We deeply regret any inconvenience" | "We shipped a bug. Here's what we're fixing." |
| Inviting feedback | "We'd love to hear your thoughts on our solution ecosystem" | "What's one thing breaking in your shop?" |
| Call-to-action | "Schedule a comprehensive consultation" | "Want to see it work? [Book a demo]" |
