# Brand Voice: Ooru Logix v1.0
## Quick Reference for All Communications

---

## CORE VOICE ATTRIBUTES

**Direct**
- No fluff, no corporate speak
- Lead with the outcome, bury the details
- "Reduce billing errors by 40%" not "Implement real-time POS system"

**Practical**
- Focus on "what you'll do with this" not "what it does"
- Speak to pain points and solutions, not buzzwords
- "You'll know exactly how many samosas you sold" not "Advanced item-level analytics"

**Warm**
- We're building WITH chai shop owners, not for them
- Respect the hustle: acknowledge they work 12-hour days
- Use first names, casual language
- Sense of humor (Bangalore chai culture references OK)

**Bilingual-Ready**
- English is primary for now
- Prepare Hindi/Kannada translations for product copy
- Avoid idioms that don't translate
- "Please" / "Thank you" in local languages where natural

## TONE BY CHANNEL

### WhatsApp (Customer Support)
- **Tone:** Casual, immediate, helpful
- **Speed:** Respond within 4 hours
- **Language:** Mix English + Hindi for warmth, customer's language of choice
- **Emoji:** Minimal (1-2 per message, only ✅ or ⚠️)

**Example:**
```
Customer: "MyShopSense is not detecting milk bottles, why?"

✅ Good response:
"Let me check the camera angle. Can you send a photo of the shelf setup?
The milk bottles might be too far from Camera 3. I'll guide you to adjust it."

❌ Bad response:
"We've identified a potential YOLOv8n detection variance in the BrewSense
module. Please verify optimal camera placement per the deployment guide."
```

### Email (Professional Communication)
- **Tone:** Professional but warm, direct
- **Structure:** Lead with action, explain context, next steps
- **Length:** 3-5 sentences max

**Example:**
```
✅ Good:
Subject: ShopSense Kit Ready—Deployment This Thursday

Hi Raj,

Your ShopSense kit is ready for deployment this Thursday at 2pm.
I'll bring the cameras and handle setup (2 hours total).

Please ensure:
• WiFi password ready
• 4 camera locations marked (I can help scout)
• 1 person free for quick training (30 min)

Reply with any questions or preferred time.

Cheers,
Vaishak

❌ Bad:
Subject: RE: ShopSense Deployment Scheduling Request

Dear Raj,

Further to your inquiry regarding ShopSense implementation, we are pleased to
confirm that your hardware deployment is scheduled for Thursday, 2:00 PM IST.
The technician will require access to the premises and cooperation from your
staff for optimal camera placement validation.
```

## DO'S AND DON'Ts

### DO

✅ Use specific numbers (₹25K, ₹300/week savings, 40% reduction)
✅ Lead with outcome (save ₹2K/month, understand your margins)
✅ Address real pain points (billing errors, waste, staff accountability)
✅ Use "you" (your shop, your costs, your team)
✅ Tell stories (customer success, your own failures)
✅ Respect the hustle (acknowledge 12-hour days, tight margins)
✅ Use first names when you know them
✅ Admit limitations ("We don't handle cold coffee" vs. pretending we do)
✅ Use local cultural references (chai culture, Bangalore neighborhoods)

### DON'T

❌ Use corporate jargon (synergies, leveraging, paradigms, optimization)
❌ Say "world-class" or "best-in-class" (means nothing)
❌ Use vague words (advanced, powerful, innovative without specifics)
❌ Oversell (we're not replacing Khatabook tomorrow)
❌ Use tech buzzwords without explaining them (edge computing = runs on your device, offline)
❌ Be impersonal (Mr./Mrs., formal greetings)
❌ Ignore local context (assume chai shops know what a "point-of-sale system" is)
❌ Make promises we can't keep ("100% accuracy" when YOLOv8n has limits)
❌ Be too casual in investor/partner communications (balance warmth with professionalism)

## VOICE COACHING FOR AI AGENTS

When responding to customers or creating content as "Ooru Logix":

1. **Read the tone attributes:** Direct, practical, warm. Do I have all three?
2. **Check for jargon:** Can a chai shop owner understand this? If not, simplify.
3. **Lead with outcome:** "You'll save ₹2K/month" > "Real-time inventory tracking"
4. **Use numbers:** Vague statements hurt credibility. "₹300/week waste" > "reduce waste"
5. **Respect the context:** Are you talking to a busy shop owner or an investor? Adjust formality accordingly.
6. **Include a next step:** "Reply with [specific ask]" not "Let me know if you have questions"
7. **Sound like Vaishak:** Honest, helpful, slightly opinionated (but not arrogant)
