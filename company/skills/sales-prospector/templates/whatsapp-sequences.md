## Guidelines for All Sequences

### Tone & Voice:
- **First message:** Warm, curious, solution-oriented (not salesy)
- **Follow-ups:** Casual, helpful, respect their time
- **Post-demo:** Enthusiastic but not pushy
- **Pilot check-ins:** Data-driven, helpful, low-pressure
- **Decision time:** Clear options, no manipulation

### Timing:
- **Day 0:** Send 2 hours after identifying lead
- **Day 3:** Send 9am (morning business is slower)
- **Day 7:** Send 8am Saturday (decision day)
- **Pilot check-ins:** Send between 8-9am (before shop gets busy)
- **Installation:** Arrive 15 min early, confirm 30 min before

### Personalization:
- Always use their name
- Reference something specific about their shop (rush time, staff size, pain they mentioned)
- Include specific metrics (accuracy %, billing time saved)
- Show you paid attention in the demo

### Response Handling:
- Reply to all messages within 4 hours (even if just "Got it, will respond fully later")
- If they ask a question, answer immediately with a direct answer + follow-up question
- If they seem hesitant, ask "What would make this a yes for you?"
- Never be pushy; offer an "out" (e.g., "No pressure, just let me know")

### Metrics to Track:
- % who respond to Day 0 message
- % who respond positively to follow-ups
- % who agree to demo after 1st message
- % who complete pilot after demo
- % who convert after pilot
- Average response time
- Average time from first message to conversion

## Do's and Don'ts

### DO:
- Personalize every message with their name & shop name
- Keep first message under 100 words
- Use "I" language ("I work with..." not "We help organizations...")
- Include specific, relevant metrics
- Offer a clear next step
- Respect their time (not sending >2 messages without response)
- Use Kannada when appropriate (shows cultural respect)

### DON'T:
- Use CAPS or excessive emojis (except in celebratory moments like Day 7)
- Copy-paste generic templates (always adapt)
- Send the same message to everyone
- Follow up more than once per 3 days
- Pitch features—pitch pain relief
- Make promises about results (say "has helped shops achieve..." not "will save you...")
- Use corporate jargon ("leverage," "synergize," "touch base")
- Send during early morning (before 8am) or late evening (after 9pm)
