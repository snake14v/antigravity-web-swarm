## 2. GST REGISTRATION & COMPLIANCE

### GST Basics for Indian Startups

**What is GST?** Goods and Services Tax (unified across India); replaces excise, service tax, VAT.
**Rate structure**: 0%, 5%, 12%, 18%, 28% (Ooru Logix products: 5% hardware, 18% SaaS)
**Registration threshold**: ₹20L annual turnover (₹10L in specific states)

### GST Registration Trigger & Process

**When: Annual turnover crosses ₹20L**

**Documents required**:
1. PAN (Permanent Account Number) - Founder's 10-digit
2. Aadhar number
3. Bank account statement (1 month recent)
4. Address proof (utility bill, lease, property tax)
5. Business description (brief 50 words)
6. Authorization letter (if filing via CA)

**Process**:
1. Visit GST portal: www.gst.gov.in
2. Choose "Registration" → "New registration"
3. Enter basic details (PAN, state, business type)
4. Upload documents
5. Email verification link (click within 24 hours)
6. Provisional registration (immediately)
7. Final registration (within 3–5 business days)

**Cost**: FREE (no GST registration fee)

**Output**:
- GSTIN (15-digit unique identifier): [State code (2) + PAN (10) + Seq (2) + Checksum (1)]
- Example: 29ABCDE1234F1Z5 (29 = Karnataka)
- Validity: Lifelong (until voluntarily cancelled)

### Post-Registration Obligations

**Monthly/Quarterly filing** (GSTR forms):

| Form | When | What | Who files |
|------|------|------|-----------|
| GSTR-1 | 11th of next month | Outward supplies (sales) | All registered |
| GSTR-3B | 20th of next month | Net tax liability | All registered |
| GSTR-2 | 15th of next month | Inward supplies (purchases) | All registered |
| GSTR-9 | 31st Dec | Annual reconciliation | All registered |

**Example filing timeline** (April–June quarter):
- 11th July: File GSTR-1 (April–June sales)
- 20th July: File GSTR-3B (April–June tax due)
- Repeat for subsequent months

### GST Calculation for Ooru Logix Products

**Hardware (ShopSense Kit)**:
- Product classification: HSN 8471.30 (Other computing devices)
- GST rate: 5%
- Invoice ex-GST: ₹25,000
- GST @ 5%: ₹1,250
- Invoice inc-GST: ₹26,250
- Input GST (on component purchases): Eligible for credit (if vendor GST registered)

**SaaS (Monthly subscription)**:
- Product classification: HSN 6110.90 (Services - ITC/BPO/Cloud)
- GST rate: 18%
- Invoice ex-GST: ₹2,000/month
- GST @ 18%: ₹360
- Invoice inc-GST: ₹2,360/month
- Input GST: Eligible on cloud, software tools, design services

**Data Insights Service**:
- Product classification: HSN 6110.90 (Business services)
- GST rate: 18%
- Invoice ex-GST: ₹500/month
- GST @ 18%: ₹90
- Invoice inc-GST: ₹590/month

### GST Reconciliation (Monthly)

**Process**:
1. Export all invoices issued (GSTR-1 data)
2. Calculate outgoing GST (sum of all invoices × tax rate)
3. Export all purchase invoices (GSTR-2 data)
4. Calculate input GST credit eligible
5. Net GST liability = Outgoing GST - Input credit
6. File GSTR-3B with payment (if liability >₹0)

**Common mistakes to avoid**:
- Filing wrong HSN code (impacts tax rate)
- Missing ITC (input GST credit) on vendor invoices
- Late filing (₹200/day penalty per return)
- Reverse charge applicability (certain B2B scenarios)

### GST Audit & Compliance

- **Annual audit requirement**: If turnover >₹1Cr in a FY
- **Suspended liability threshold**: If GST liability >₹10L in month, central bank account may be blocked (must file return to unblock)
- **Notices**: If GST filed <expected (red flag for income tax authorities)

## 3. MSME & STARTUP INDIA REGISTRATION

### MSME Classification (Udyam Portal)

**Eligibility**: Any business with investment in plant/machinery or turnover criteria

**Classifications** (As per amended definition, effective July 2020):

| Category | Investment in plant/machinery | Annual Turnover |
|----------|---|---|
| Micro | <₹25L | <₹5Cr |
| Small | ₹25L–₹5Cr | ₹5Cr–₹25Cr |
| Medium | ₹5Cr–₹10Cr | ₹25Cr–₹100Cr |

**Ooru Logix likely falls into Micro** (investment in hardware <₹25L, turnover <₹5Cr for next 2 years)

**Registration process** (Udyam):
1. Visit www.udyamregistration.gov.in
2. Enter Aadhar + PAN
3. Fill business details
4. Self-certification (no approval needed)
5. Receive Udyam ID immediately

**Cost**: FREE
**Documents**: None required (self-certification)
**Time**: 15 minutes

**Benefits**:
- **Credit Guarantee Scheme**: Up to ₹100L loan without collateral (CGTMSE)
- **Priority Sector Lending**: Banks prioritize MSME loans (lower interest 2–3% discount)
- **Patent/Trademark subsidy**: 50% rebate on patent filing (under Startup India)
- **Govt tender eligibility**: Can participate in public procurement
- **Tax holidays**: Some states offer turnover tax exemption
- **Market linkage**: Government e-procurement portal access

**Recommendation**: Register at Udyam immediately (no cost; high benefit)

### Startup India Registration (DPIIT)

**Eligibility**:
- Incorporated entity (LLP or Pvt Ltd; not applicable to sole proprietor initially)
- <7 years old (from incorporation)
- Annual turnover <₹100Cr
- Working towards innovation/technology development

**Benefits**:
- **Tax holiday**: 100% exemption on profits (1st 3 FY out of 7 years)
- **Patent fast-tracking**: Patent filed gets expedited examination
- **Patent fee waiver**: 80% reduction on filing fees
- **Startup India seed fund**: Access to SISBF (₹10L–₹1Cr grants; highly competitive)
- **Regulatory simplification**: Self-certification for labor, environmental laws
- **Government procurement**: Can participate without prior turnover requirement
- **Brand building**: Listed on Startup India portal (visibility)

**Registration process**:
1. Incorporate as Pvt Ltd / LLP first
2. Visit Startup India portal: www.startupindia.gov.in
3. Upload incorporation certificate + PAN + identity proof
4. Self-certification (recognition)
5. Receive Startup India Recognition Certificate (digital)

**Cost**: FREE
**Time**: 3–5 business days
**Retention**: Lifelong (even after 7 years if turnover <₹100Cr)

**Action**: Register post-incorporation (when converting to LLP/Pvt Ltd)

## 4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY (IP) PROTECTION

### Trademark: ShopSense Brand

**Current status**: Brand name used without formal protection

**Trademark registration process**:
1. **Class selection**: Class 35 (retail services), Class 42 (software/SaaS), Class 09 (hardware/computing devices)
2. **Search**: Check existing trademarks (www.ipindia.gov.in)
   - Verify "ShopSense" not already registered in Classes 09, 35, 42
3. **Application filing**:
   - File form TM-A with IPO (IP Office, India)
   - Cost: ₹9,000 per class (~₹27K for 3 classes)
   - Timeline: 4–6 weeks for examination
4. **Examination**: IPO reviews for objections
   - If objections: Respond within 2 months
   - If approved: Publish in trademark journal (3 months public notice)
5. **Registration**: Issue registration certificate (valid 10 years; renewable)

**Process timeline**: 12–18 months (average)
**Advantages of registration**:
- Exclusive rights in India for that word/logo
- Legal recourse against infringement
- Export market recognition (IP right)
- Asset value (can be licensed, sold)

**Cost**: ₹9,000–15,000 per class + ₹3,000–5,000 CA fees = ~₹35,000 total

**Recommendation**: File trademark application post-Gate 2 (once product-market fit proven)

### Patent: Edge AI Billing Method (if invented)

**Example**: If Ooru Logix develops novel AI algorithm for real-time billing optimization at edge

**Patent process**:
1. **Prior art search**: Check if invention already exists (Google Patents, IPO database)
2. **Provisional application**: File with IPO (gives priority date, low cost)
   - Cost: ₹1,500 filing fee
   - Duration: 12 months to file complete application
   - Timeline: 1–2 weeks
3. **Complete patent application**: Filed within 12 months of provisional
   - Cost: ₹8,000–12,000 + CA fees ₹5,000–10,000
4. **Examination**: IPO examines (4–6 months)
5. **Publication**: Published in patent journal (3 months after examination)
6. **Grant**: Patent certificate issued (valid 20 years from filing date)

**Patent timeline**: 3–5 years (average in India)
**Startup India benefit**: Fast-track examination (6 months instead of 2+ years)

**Cost**: ₹15,000–25,000 + CA fees = ~₹40,000
**Recommendation**: File provisional patent early if core technology is proprietary

## 6. DATA PRIVACY & DPDP ACT 2023 COMPLIANCE

### DPDP Act 2023 Overview (India's data protection law)

**Effective date**: August 4, 2023 (notified; implementation in phases)

**Key requirements**:

| Requirement | Description | Ooru Logix Impact |
|-------------|---|---|
| **Consent** | Must obtain explicit consent for data collection | Seek customer consent in MSA |
| **Purpose limitation** | Data used only for stated purpose | Limit data to service delivery + support |
| **Data minimization** | Collect only necessary data | Don't ask for optional info |
| **Retention limit** | Retain only as long as needed | Delete camera footage after 24 months |
| **Security** | Implement reasonable security measures | Use encryption, access control |
| **User rights** | Users can access, correct, delete data | Provide self-service portal |
| **Breach notification** | Notify users within 72 hours of data breach | Document breach response process |
| **DPA (Grievance Officer)** | Appoint data protection officer or grievance handler | Designate internal POC |

### Camera Footage Specific (Critical for Ooru Logix)

**Classification**: Camera footage = Personal data (per DPDP, as it may capture faces/identifying info)

**Compliance checklist**:

- [ ] **Consent**: Customer agrees (via MSA) to camera data collection
- [ ] **Purpose**: Clearly state cameras used for [retail analytics, inventory, security]
- [ ] **Employee notice**: Customer must inform employees/visitors about cameras
- [ ] **Retention**: Set default retention to 24 months; customer can adjust
- [ ] **Access control**: Only customer + support (with customer approval) can view footage
- [ ] **Encryption**: Footage encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3)
- [ ] **Deletion**: One-click deletion option in web dashboard
- [ ] **Export**: Customer can export footage (portability right)
- [ ] **Cross-border transfer**: If AWS US-based, disclose to customer upfront

| 10+ employees | Mandatory | Mandatory (if salary <₹21K) |

**PF contribution** (once triggered):
- Employee: 10% of basic salary (withheld from salary)
- Company: 10% of basic salary (company contribution)
- Example: ₹15K salary → PF ₹1,500 (employee) + ₹1,500 (company) = ₹3,000/month

**ESI contribution** (if salary <₹21K):
- Employee: 0.75% of wages
- Company: 3.25% of wages
- Example: ₹15K salary → ESI ₹113 (employee) + ₹488 (company) = ₹600/month

**Action items** (trigger at first hire):
- [ ] Register with EPFO (Employees' Provident Fund Organization)
- [ ] Register with ESIC (Employees' State Insurance Corporation)
- [ ] Submit monthly PF/ESI contributions + returns
- [ ] Conduct quarterly salary audits (compliance check)

## 9. REGULATORY WATCHLIST & COMPLIANCE CALENDAR

### BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards) Certification

**Requirement**: Electronics products may require BIS certification

**Products affected**:
- Raspberry Pi board + camera + components: May fall under "Computing devices" (IEC 61000 series)
- Power supply: Likely needs BIS mark (electronic equipment)

**Compliance**:
- Self-certification path available for startups (mark of conformity)
- Cost: ₹2,000–5,000 per product type
- Timeline: 4–6 weeks

**Action**: Engage BIS consultant once mass production starts (Gate 3, 5+ customers)

**Optional but useful**:
- Test lab accreditation (validate hardware quality)
- Certification for export markets
- Cost: ₹15,000–30,000

**Action**: Consider once revenue >₹50L/year

### MEITY (Ministry of Electronics & Information Technology)

**Guidelines to monitor**:
- Export controls (if selling internationally)
- Data residency (MEITY prefers India-hosted data for govt customers)
- Cybersecurity standards (ISO 27001, if targeting enterprise)

**Action**: Monitor if pursuing govt contracts (lower priority for initial gates)

### RoHS/WEEE Compliance (If exporting)

**Restriction of Hazardous Substances** (EU regulation, also adopted in India):
- Limit lead, mercury, cadmium in electronics
- Ooru Logix hardware likely compliant (RPi, cameras standard)
- Compliance cost: Covered by component suppliers

**Action**: Verify with PCB/assembly partners; declare compliance on invoices
