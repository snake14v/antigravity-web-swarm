## POLICY STATEMENT

**Official Declaration:**

> As of April 1, 2026, Ooru Logix is a single-product company. That product is **ShopSense**.
>
> All other initiatives are either frozen, paused, or deferred. No new products launch until ShopSense achieves product-market fit (defined as 1 paying customer, then 5 paying customers, then 20 customers). Every hour spent on non-ShopSense work is an hour not spent proving this core bet works.

This policy enforces a deliberate constraint: focus creates efficiency. Distraction kills startups.

## PRODUCT STATUS DEFINITIONS

### ACTIVE
- Receiving full development and commercial attention
- Vaishak's primary 80% time allocation
- Revenue target is mandatory
- Customer interviews are required
- Monthly progress reviews

### FROZEN
- No new features or product development
- Maintenance only (bugfixes, uptime, security)
- Can resume development only after **Gate 3: 1 paying ShopSense customer**
- Existing customers supported

### PAUSED
- No active development or marketing
- Inbound inquiries only (no outbound pursuit)
- Customer support if inbound appears
- Can resume only after **Gate 4: 5 paying ShopSense customers**
- Maximum 20% time allocation if customer appears

### DEFERRED
- Not on the roadmap
- Mentioned in strategy, not being built
- Earliest resume date is 2027 or later
- Budget not allocated

### SUNSET
- Being phased out
- Active communication with users about transition
- Support window has explicit end date

## DECISION RULES: WHEN SOMEONE WANTS TO ADD SOMETHING NEW

When a customer, collaborator, or Vaishak himself proposes a new product, feature, or initiative:

### Rule 1: Does it directly serve ShopSense?
**YES** → Build it. Prioritize immediately.
Examples: hardware mounting system for chai shop walls, SMS notifications for low stock, integration with existing tills.

### Rule 2: Does it generate revenue this month?
**YES** → Consider carefully. If it's a real inbound opportunity, allocate max 20% time. Execute ruthlessly.
**NO** → Go to Rule 3.

### Rule 3: Is it interesting/cool?
**YES** → Write it down in **BACKLOG.md** with context, do NOT start building immediately.
**NO** → Reject politely.

### Rule 4: Is it an emergency?
Security breach? Legal issue? Existing customer in crisis? → Handle immediately and document.

## EXCEPTION PROCESS: BREAKING THIS POLICY

Vaishak retains the right to break this policy when strategic. But it must be deliberate, not accidental.

**If Vaishak wants to spend time on non-ShopSense work:**

1. **Write it down** in PROGRESS.md with:
   - What work is being done
   - Why it matters
   - Expected time allocation
   - Expected outcome

2. **Ask the forcing question:**
   > "Does this work serve ShopSense pilots in the next 30 days?"
   - If YES → Break policy, document it, proceed
   - If NO → Do not break policy. Wait until the Gates are hit.

3. **Review the decision:**
   - After the work is done, assess in PROGRESS.md whether the time was well spent
   - Use this to calibrate future exception decisions

**Example of Valid Exception:**
> "Repairing the OLOG Platform database (8 hours of work). Reason: An inbound inquiry from a potential web design client who mentioned they might be interested in ShopSense pilots if the platform is stable. This work directly impacts the 30-day ShopSense pipeline."

**Example of Invalid Exception:**
> "Building new features for AurumGuard MK II hardware." (No ShopSense connection in next 30 days. Do not start.)
