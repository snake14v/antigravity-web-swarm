# Ooru Logix — Go-To-Market Strategy

**Product:** ShopSense (edge AI retail hardware)
**Price:** ₹25,000 per unit
**Target Market:** Bangalore chai shops (₹8-12L annual turnover)
**Go-to-market approach:** Direct founder sales + viral growth loops

---

## 1. Beachhead Market Definition

### Geographic Focus
- **Primary:** Bangalore, within 3 km radius of Vaishak's current location
- **Density target:** 8–15 chai shops (chai angadis, small kiosks, street-front tea stalls)
- **Why Bangalore first:** Founder lives here, understands the ecosystem, can physically visit and support customers

### Shop Profile
- **Annual turnover:** ₹8–12L (small independent operators)
- **Daily footfall:** 60–150 customers
- **Current payment system:** Cash-only or simple POS (if any)
- **Owner profile:** Age 35–55, runs 1–3 outlets, tech-skeptical but profit-hungry
- **Key pain:** Stock loss (₹300–400/day), no data on what sells, manual inventory, frequent disputes with staff

---

## 2. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Characteristics
1. **High-loss shop** — Owner reports 10–15% daily shrinkage (₹100–200/day)
2. **Profit-conscious** — Closely watches margins, knows his numbers
3. **Early adopter within SMB** — Not first to tech, but willing to try if ROI is clear
4. **Staff trust issues** — Suspects cashier/staff of skimming; wants accountability
5. **Accessible location** — Within Bangalore, willing to meet the founder
6. **Growth mindset** — Thinking about "what if I could operate better?"

### Red Flags (Avoid)
- Large chain (Chaayos, Chai Point) — Different buying motion, already have POS
- Ultra-budget shops (<₹5L annual) — Can't afford ₹25K
- Tech-forward shops — Already have inventory systems, less pain
- Defensive owner — Sees tech as threat to jobs, won't engage

## 3. Sales Motion: Cold Intro → Deployed → Paid

### Phase 1: Cold Walk-In (2–5 min)
**Goal:** Get a 15-minute conversation scheduled

**Script (Founder)**
> "Hi, I'm Vaishak. I built a small AI camera for chai shops — it tracks what customers buy without cameras in the face. Cuts down on stock loss, helps you understand your business better. It's ₹25K all-in. Takes 10 minutes to set up. Can I show you in 5 minutes how it works?"

**Positioning:** Not technology → Results (₹300+/day recovery)

### Phase 2: Live Demo at Shop (15–20 min)
**What to show:**
1. Physical hardware (sleek, fits on shelf)
2. 2-min video of another shop's live footage (anonymized)
3. The simple output: "Yesterday you sold 47 chai, 12 samosa, 8 lassi" (no faces, just items)
4. The math: "If 10% of those weren't rung up, that's ₹150/day = ₹45K/year"

**Objection handling (in Phase 2):**
- *"Too expensive"* → "You lose that in 80 days of shrinkage. What if we recover just half?"
- *"I don't trust cameras"* → "No faces, no recordings. Just: item, quantity, time. That's it."
- *"What if it breaks?"* → "We come fix it. Free support for 1 year. Hardware is indestructible."
- *"I need to think about it"* → "I'll leave a unit for 2 weeks free. Try it, see the data. No pressure."

### Phase 3: Free Pilot (14 days)
**Offer:** Deploy hardware, collect 2 weeks of baseline data (no payment, no commitment)

**Shop owner gets:**
- Physical device on-site
- Daily SMS summaries of items sold
- Call from Vaishak on day 7 to review data together
- Written report: "Here's what you lost to shrinkage in 2 weeks"

**Vaishak gets:**
- Permission to track the unit's performance
- Photo for portfolio (with permission)
- Testimonial for next sales pitch
- Real-world validation of edge AI accuracy

### Phase 4: Conversion (Day 14)
**Decision point:** Owner sees 2 weeks of data

**If data is compelling** (e.g., "You're missing ₹250/week"):
- "Will you take it? Easiest investment you'll make."
- Collect ₹25K (cash, bank transfer, or installment: ₹8,500 × 3 months, no interest)
- Install properly, train staff
- Ongoing: monthly check-in call

**If data is weak** (e.g., "Looks like my loss is lower than I thought"):
- Acknowledge it
- Offer a smaller package: "Maybe start with the entry-level version (₹12K)" (future product)
- Stay in touch; revisit in 6 months

## 4. Pricing Strategy

### Why ₹25,000?
- **Hardware cost:** ₹13,750 (55% of price)
- **Gross margin:** 45% = ₹11,250 per unit
- **ROI for shop:** Breaks even in 80 days (assuming ₹300+/day loss recovery)
- **Price positioning:** High enough to feel credible ("serious investment"), low enough for SMB affordability

### When to Offer Discounts
1. **Volume commitment:** Buy 3+ units → ₹22,500 each (10% discount)
2. **Referral bonus:** Refer another shop, both get ₹500 off
3. **Early adopter** (first 5 units): "You're helping validate the product" → ₹20,000
4. **Multi-location operator:** "Consolidate data from all outlets" → Custom pricing

### Payment Terms
- **Standard:** Full payment upfront (cash, bank transfer, Stripe)
- **For hesitant customers:** 3 installments of ₹8,500 (0% interest, no formal contract needed)
- **For strong advocates:** Buy now, pay in 30 days (builds loyalty)

### Future Upsell
- **Year 2:** ₹50/month SaaS layer (cloud analytics, AI recommendations)
- **Year 3:** "ShopSense Pro" with better camera, larger FOV → ₹40,000

## 5. Distribution Channels (Priority Order)

### Tier 1: Direct Sales (Founder)
**Channels:** Walking into shops, founder's personal network
**Conversion rate:** 5–10% (assuming good ICP targeting)
**Time per deal:** 3 weeks (demo → pilot → close)
**Capacity:** Vaishak can close ~2 units/month (hands-on)
**Timeline to scale:** Need BD hire by month 3 to go beyond 2–3 units/month

### Tier 2: LinkedIn Inbound
**Strategy:** Founder posts about chai shop economics, AI retail, loss prevention
**Content loop:**
- "Why we built ShopSense" (problem narrative)
- "Real data from a Bangalore chai shop" (proof)
- "How AI catches shrinkage" (explainer)
- "Join 5 chai shops using ShopSense" (social proof)

**Expected:** 2–3 inbound inquiries/month after 2 months of consistent posting
**Conversion:** Higher (self-qualified) → 15–20%

### Tier 3: GitHub Open Source Community
**Strategy:** Release edge AI inference code (YOLOv8n + custom item classifier) as open-source
**Benefit:** Brand as AI-first, attract developer interest, PDR mentions
**Conversion path:**
- Developer deploys YOLOv8n locally → sees value
- "Want this packaged + hardware support?" → ₹25K offer
- Niche: tech-forward chai shop owners or restaurant operators

### Tier 4: Referral Loop
**Mechanics:** First customer refers second → ₹500 commission (or ₹500 discount for both)
**Why it works:** Chai shop owners know other shop owners
**Timeline:** Kicks in by month 4–5 (after 3–4 paid customers)

### Tier 5: Channel Partners (Year 2)
**Target partners:**
- FMCG distributors (e.g., Britannia, Nestlé tea distributors)
- POS vendors (Razorpay Shop, Square)
- Business consulting firms working with SMBs

**Model:** "Sell ShopSense to your retail customers for ₹25K; your commission is ₹3,500 per unit"
**Volume lever:** Not needed in Year 1, but essential for scaling to 1,000+ shops

## 6. The Demo Script

### Setting: Inside a chai shop. Owner is behind the counter.

**You:** "Hi, do you have 5 minutes? I want to show you something that might help your business."

**Owner (if interested):** "Okay, but make it quick."

**You:** "So here's the deal. On average, small chai shops lose ₹200–400 every single day. Customers don't pay, staff forget to ring it up, someone pours an extra chai by mistake. Nobody knows. Right?"

**Owner:** "Yeah, it happens."

**You:** "Exactly. I built this small camera system. It sits here [point to counter], watches what people order, and tells you exactly what you sold every day. Helps you catch the gaps."

[Show demo unit or phone with live feed from another shop]

**You:** "No faces, no recordings. Just: Chai × 3, Samosa × 2, Lassi × 1. That's it. Gives you a number: 'Yesterday, you sold ₹3,200 of stuff. Rung up ₹3,000. Missing: ₹200.' Do that every day, and you recover ₹6,000/month."

**Owner:** "How much does it cost?"

**You:** "₹25,000. Takes 5 minutes to set up. I'll handle the tech part. You just let it sit and watch the data come in every morning as an SMS."

**Owner:** "That's expensive."

**You:** "Fair. But think about it: you recover ₹200/day, that's ₹6,000/month. In 4 months, it pays for itself. After that, it's pure upside. Plus, you know what actually sells — maybe you should have more samosas, fewer kachori. Helps you stock smarter."

**Owner (warming up):** "Hmm. What if it breaks?"

**You:** "We fix it. Free support for the first year. And we own the margin risk — if it's wrong, we improve it. You don't pay for broken hardware."

**Owner:** "Let me think about it."

**You:** "I get it. Here's an idea: let me leave this unit here for 2 weeks. No payment, no commitment. Just use it normally. I'll show you the data on day 7. If you hate it, I pick it up. If you like it, we talk price. Deal?"

**Owner:** "Okay, sure. Can't hurt."

## 7. Objection Handling Deep-Dive

| Objection | Root Cause | Response | Proof |
|-----------|-----------|----------|-------|
| "Too expensive" | Price shock, unfamiliar investment category | "You lose this in 80 days of shrinkage. Let's prove it with a free pilot." | Show math on phone: ₹300/day × 80 = ₹24K |
| "I don't need this" | Low awareness of shrinkage | "Most shop owners think they lose ₹50/day. Our first 3 customers found ₹250–400/day. Let's find yours." | Free pilot removes risk |
| "What if it breaks?" | Trust in reliability, fear of sunk cost | "Free support 1 year. We own the uptime risk. And it's built for chai shops — dust, heat, moisture-proof." | Show durability (drop test video) |
| "I'll do it manually" | Overconfidence in current system | "You could. Most try. But you'll spot-check 5% of transactions. This catches 100% in the blind spots." | Show false-negative examples |
| "My staff won't like it" | Fear of surveillance backlash | "Tell them it helps you manage smarter, not spy on them. Staff in shops using ShopSense actually prefer it — less blame games." | Get testimonial from first customer |
| "Privacy concerns" | Data ownership, government worry | "Zero faces, zero identifiable info. Just items. We don't store it in cloud. Data stays on your device." | Demo: show the actual output (no PII) |
| "Show me proof first" | Reasonable skepticism | "Absolutely. 2-week free trial. Collect real data from your shop. Then decide." | Trial removes all risk |

## 8. Expansion Motion: Basic → Pro → Enterprise

### Year 1: Basic (ShopSense v1)
- Single device per shop
- Item-level tracking only
- SMS daily summary
- ₹25,000 one-time
- Support: email + phone

### Year 2: Pro (ShopSense Pro)
- Multi-device support (consolidate 2–3 locations)
- Staff-level tracking (who served, how long)
- Cloud dashboard (see trends, comparisons)
- Predictive insights: "You'll run out of chai in 2 hours"
- ₹40,000 hardware + ₹50/month SaaS
- Support: priority email + monthly calls

### Year 3: Enterprise (ShopSense for Chains)
- 10+ locations, consolidated analytics
- API for POS integration (Razorpay, Square, Juspay)
- Bulk reorder automation
- Staff scheduling recommendations
- ₹50,000/location + ₹200/month SaaS + ₹5,000 integration fee
- Dedicated account manager

## 13. Risks & Mitigation

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Hardware malfunction in field | Lost revenue, broken trust | 2-unit inventory buffer, remote diagnostics, 24h replacement guarantee |
| Shop owner abandons during pilot | Wasted time | Daily check-ins by Vaishak, SMS reminders, offer help troubleshooting |
| Market says "don't need this" | Pivot needed | Honest conversations, measure shrinkage in pilot, validate before scaling |
| Founder bottleneck at 2–3 units/month | Can't scale | Hire BD person by month 3, arm them with script + case studies |
| Regulatory (data storage, PII) | Legal exposure | Zero PII stored, on-device only, privacy-first architecture documented |
