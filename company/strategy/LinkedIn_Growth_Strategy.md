## PART 4: LinkedIn Post Formats

### Format 1: Carousel (5-6 slides)
**Why:** Algorithm favors, high engagement, visual + text

**Structure:**
1. Hook (big claim or question)
2-4. Supporting evidence / steps / data
5. Conclusion + insight
6. CTA (ask for comment, DM, share)

**Best for:** Educational posts, comparisons, how-to, data-driven

**Example:**
```
[Slide 1] "Why edge AI beats cloud in 3 ways"
[Slide 2] Cost comparison (chart)
[Slide 3] Latency comparison (chart)
[Slide 4] Privacy advantage (diagram)
[Slide 5] Conclusion + "Which matters most to you?"
[Slide 6] GitHub link
```

---

### Format 2: Long-Form Text Post
**Why:** Builds authority, storytelling, depth

**Structure:**
- Hook (2-3 lines, compelling)
- Body (8-12 lines, narrative or steps)
- Insight (1-2 lines, takeaway)
- CTA (clear action)

**Best for:** Personal stories, reflections, lessons learned, advice

**Example:**
```
"I spent a week in a chai shop in Koramangala.

Here's what I learned about why AI startups fail:

We build for ourselves, not for customers. The shop owner
didn't want a 'smart inventory system.' They wanted 5 hours
back in their week.

That's the difference between a failed product and ShopSense.

What's a customer insight that changed your work?
```

---

### Format 3: Document Post
**Why:** Unusual format, high engagement, looks professional

**How to:** Write in Word/Google Docs, export as image (screenshot), post as carousel

**Best for:** Case studies, playbooks, analysis, frameworks

---

### Format 4: Video Post
**Why:** Highest engagement (3x text), ideal for demos

**Formats:**
- 15-30 second demo (auto-plays in feed)
- Talking head (you, speaking to camera)
- Screen recording (technical walkthrough)

**Best for:** Product demos, founder talks, tutorials
