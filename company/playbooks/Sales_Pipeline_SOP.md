# Sales Pipeline SOP — ShopSense

Standard Operating Procedure for converting shops to paying customers. Target: 15 units deployed in 6 months via brutal beachhead strategy (start narrow, go deep).

---

## Stage 1: Identification

Finding the right shops to target. Time: Day 0 (before contact)

### Target Shop Profile (ICP)

Use this checklist to evaluate if a shop is a good prospect:

**Essential Criteria (ALL must be YES):**

- [ ] **High volume counter retail:** Visible stock of cigarettes, beverages, snacks, convenience items behind counter
  - Indicator: Queue forms at counter during rush hours
  - Verification: Walk by 8-11 AM, 1-3 PM, 5-7 PM — do customers wait?

- [ ] **Independent owner-operated:** NOT franchise, NOT chain store, NOT corporate POS
  - Ask directly: "Is this your shop?" (if yes, proceed)
  - If chain/franchise: Skip this shop (HQ won't approve pilots easily)

- [ ] **Manual billing system:** Still using cash register or paper + pen
  - Observation: No scanner, no computerized POS visible
  - Indicator: Takes 30+ seconds per transaction

- [ ] **No existing POS/scanner:** Not already using billing software
  - Ask: "Do you use any computer system for billing?" (if no, proceed)
  - If they have POS: Skip (different pitch, different customer success path)

- [ ] **Accessible power & WiFi:** Counter has space for equipment
  - Power outlet within 2m of counter: required for Pi + touchscreen
  - WiFi signal at counter: can you get 3+ bars on phone?
  - Counter space: minimum 30cm W × 20cm D available

**Revenue Size (Target sweet spot):**

- Monthly turnover: ₹40,000 - ₹20,00,000/month
  - Below ₹40K: Shop owner might not care about ₹300/day savings
  - Above ₹20L: Likely already using POS or franchise

- Estimation method: Watch during peak hour (say, 7-8 AM)
  - Count transactions: X
  - Estimate bill size: ₹200 (cigarettes/beverage)
  - 1 hour × X × ₹200 = hourly revenue
  - Rough monthly: hourly × 8 (open hours) × 25 (days)

**Location (Geographic concentration):**

- Same neighborhood: build density for support efficiency
- Target neighborhoods in Bangalore: Brigade Road, Koramangala, Whitefield, Indiranagar (early pilots)
- Avoid scattered pilots across the city (support becomes impossible for 1 person)

### Identification Workflow

**Step 1: Select neighborhood** (e.g., Brigade Road, Koramangala)

**Step 2: Walk the strip**
- Walk the main road and all side lanes
- Note every shop that matches ICP (high volume, independent, manual billing)
- Use your phone to mark location on map + take photo of storefront

**Step 3: Create prospect list**
- Google Sheet with columns:
  - [ ] Shop name
  - [ ] Address (exact)
  - [ ] Owner name (if known from sign)
  - [ ] Estimated monthly revenue
  - [ ] Visible items (cigarettes, beverages, etc.)
  - [ ] Counter type (open, enclosed, etc.)
  - [ ] POS status (manual or computerized)
  - [ ] Power/WiFi available (yes/no/uncertain)

**Step 4: Rank by priority**
- Criteria: High visibility + visible volume + good power/WiFi location
- Sort to: Target top 5 shops in your neighborhood

## Stage 2: First Contact

Getting the conversation started. Time: Day 1-3 (after identification)

### Pre-Contact Preparation

Before walking into the shop:

- [ ] Review shop details (owner name, exact location, visible products)
- [ ] Prepare demo video on your phone (2-3 min ShopSense demo)
- [ ] Print 1-pager (ShopSense one-pager, A4, fold to fit pocket)
- [ ] Charge phone, bring business card
- [ ] Prepare pitch in local language (Kannada/Hindi preferred)

### Timing & Approach

**Best time to approach:**
- Off-peak hours: 10 AM - 12 PM (not during rush)
- NOT during 7-9 AM (breakfast rush), 12-2 PM (lunch), 5-7 PM (evening rush)

**Location:**
- Walk in calmly, don't hover around (raises suspicion)
- Approach owner/manager directly (not junior staff)
- Be respectful — you're interrupting their day

### Opening Script (Adapt to language & shop style)

**In English (if appropriate):**

"Namaskar [Owner Name]. I'm Vaishak, from Ooru Logix. I noticed you're very busy here, especially during rush hours. I make a product for shops like yours that actually works — it reduces billing errors and recovers lost revenue. Takes 2 minutes to show. Interested?"

**In Kannada (if appropriate):**

"Namaskara [Hesaru]. Nanu Ooru Logix-ada Vaishak. Ninage shop-alli busy avutte bere notice madi. Nanu avaru heege billing errors reduce maduvuda product madi. Two minutes-alli kanapisuttini. Interested-a?"

**Key elements:**
- Your name + company name (builds credibility)
- Observation about their shop (shows you've paid attention)
- Specific benefit (billing errors, lost revenue)
- Time scarcity (2 minutes, respect their time)
- Ask for permission ("Interested?")

### If Interested (Next 10-15 minutes)

**A: Show demo video** (if they have time)
- Pull up 2-3 min demo video on your phone
- Stand beside them, show counter detection in real-time
- Point out: speed of detection, accuracy, touchscreen ease
- Ask: "Does this look useful for your shop?"

**OR B: Offer full demo appointment**
- If they're too busy: "I can't show you properly now. Can I come back tomorrow at 10 AM with a demo unit?"

**Either way, before you leave:**
- Ask: "Does this solve something real for you?"
- Get their WhatsApp number (say: "I'll send you updates")
- Give them one-pager (explain: all info, my number, examples)
- Take their name clearly (spell it, confirm)

### If Not Interested (Still important)

**Don't pitch harder.** Instead:

"No problem! I understand. If you change your mind or know anyone who might benefit, here's my number. Good luck with your shop!"

- Leave one-pager
- Save their WhatsApp (follow up in 3 months)
- Mark as "Not Interested — [reason]" in CRM

## Stage 3: Demo

Showing how ShopSense solves their specific problem. Time: Day 3-7 (3-4 days after first contact)

### Demo Setup Options

**Option A: On-site demo (10-15 min at their counter)**
- Bring demo unit (pre-loaded Pi, cameras, touchscreen)
- Set up quickly at a clear spot
- Run 20-30 transactions
- Show accuracy & speed
- Takes up their counter space — only if they agree

**Option B: Visit a running unit (customer testimonial)**
- Better option: invite owner to see a live unit at another shop
- Shows real performance, real customer testimonial
- 30 min visit, including customer conversation
- Shop owner hears from peer (more convincing than you)

**Recommendation:** Use Option B if you have a customer willing to host. Otherwise, Option A.

### Demo Agenda (15 minutes)

**Minute 0-2: Setup**
- Power on unit
- Explain the 4 cameras: over counter, sides, cash
- Show touchscreen: "This is what staff see all day"

**Minute 2-8: Live demo**
- Have shop owner pick 10-15 items from their own shop
- Ring them up slowly, one by one
- Point out detection on screen: "See it captured that ₹20 cigarette pack?"
- Show confidence score: "85% confident — high accuracy"
- Deliberately miss 1 item: show they can manually add it ("What if something's missed?")

**Minute 8-12: Accuracy & value**
- "In 2 weeks, we'll know exactly how many errors you have now"
- "Most shops see 15-20% error rate. ShopSense gets it to <5%"
- Quick math: "If you do ₹2,000/day in sales, 15% errors = ₹300/day loss. ShopSense recovers that."

**Minute 12-15: Ease of use & next steps**
- "Staff learns in 1 day. No training needed."
- Ask key question: "Does this look like something that would work in your shop?"
- If YES: "I'd like to bring it for a 2-week free trial. No payment, no commitment. Just data to help both of us."

### Soft Close Script

**If they hesitate:**

"Look, I get it. New tech, concerns about cost, whether it'll actually work. The best way to know is a trial. You use it free for 2 weeks. You see real data. Then decide. Sound fair?"

**If they say no:**
"Okay. What would need to change for you to say yes? Would another week of data help? Or do you want to see it at another shop first?"

## Stage 4: Pilot (2-week trial)

### Pilot Timeline Overview

- **Day 0:** Installation + staff training
- **Day 1:** First check-in (8 AM call)

- **Days 8-14:** Gather ROI evidence, prepare conversion conversation

## Stage 5: Conversion

The close. Time: Days 21-25 (3-5 days after pilot ends)

### Conversion Conversation (Script)

**Call them on Day 14-15 evening:**

"Hi [Owner]. I wanted to review the pilot data with you. Do you have 15 minutes tomorrow morning?"

**Schedule call for morning. Then on the call:**

"Okay [Owner]. We ran ShopSense for 2 weeks. Here's what the data shows:

📊 **The Numbers**
- Total transactions detected: 620
- Accuracy: 92% (you avoided errors on 571 items)
- False positives: 12 (rare, easy to fix manually)
- System uptime: 99.5% (was running when you needed it)

💰 **Your Savings**
- Errors prevented: ~50/week (conservatively)
- Per error cost: ₹50 (average item value)
- Weekly recovery: ₹2,500 (your billable mistakes that would've been lost)
- Monthly recovery: ₹10,000 (4.8 weeks)
- Payback period: 25,000 ÷ 10,000 = 2.5 months

That's solid. You make your money back in 2.5 months, and profit ₹120K+ next year.

📋 **What I'm Proposing**
Option 1: Make it permanent. I install it properly, you pay ₹25,000 one-time. I support it free for 6 months, then ₹500/month if you want updates + priority support.

Option 2: Return it today. But you go back to losing ₹300+/day like before.

**What makes sense for Fresh Mart?"**

**Wait for their response. Don't fill silence.**

### Their Likely Responses

**If YES:**

"Excellent! I'm happy. Let me send you an invoice now. Once payment clears, I'll schedule the final installation. You'll have it running permanently by [date]."

**Immediate next steps:**
1. Send invoice via WhatsApp/email (ShopSense_Invoice_Template.pdf)
2. Confirm UPI/NEFT details
3. Once payment received, schedule permanent installation (Day 0 of post-conversion support)
4. Move to "Closed - Won" in pipeline

**If "I need to think about it":**

"I understand. But here's the thing — I only have one demo unit. If I don't hear from you by [DATE: 3 days], I'll need to move it to another shop. So I need to know by then. Fair?"

**Set a hard deadline.** No open-ended pilot extensions. Either they're convinced or they're not.

**If NO:**

"I understand it's a big decision. What would change your mind? Is it the price? Is it concerns about reliability? Help me understand."

**Listen. Don't push. Collect feedback:**
- Price too high? (don't discount on first ask)
- Worried about accuracy? ("Let me improve the model more")
- Need more time? ("I can't extend the trial, but...")
- Want to see it elsewhere first? ("I can arrange that")

## Pipeline Health Rules

**How to know if your pipeline is stuck:**

| Milestone | Ideal Timeline | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|
| Identification → First Contact | Within 7 days | >7 days: you're overthinking |
| First Contact → Demo | Within 14 days | >14 days: they've lost interest, move on |
| Demo → Pilot agreement | Within 7 days | >7 days: they're stalling, they're a "no" |
| Pilot → Conversion conversation | Day 14 | >Day 15: call immediately, don't wait |
| Conversion conversation → Payment | Within 7 days | >7 days: move to "Lost", don't chase |

**Weekly review rule:**
- Every Monday, review your pipeline
- Any shop >red flag timeline → take action (call, visit, move to Lost)
- Don't let shops languish in any stage

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

**Anti-pattern 1: Spending too long on early-stage demos**
- If a shop hasn't decided after 2 demos → move on. They're not ready. Life's short.

**Anti-pattern 2: Extending pilots beyond 14 days**
- Pilots should end on Day 14. Period. "One more week" = delay, uncertainty, loss.

**Anti-pattern 3: Negotiating the price too much**
- Stand firm on ₹25K for permanent installation. If they want a discount, they don't believe in the value.
- Only discount if you add services (extended support, model training, etc.)

**Anti-pattern 4: Chasing "maybes" instead of finding "yeses"**
- Talk to 10 shops to find 2-3 that say yes. Don't spend months trying to convince 1 maybe.

**Anti-pattern 5: Not moving rejected pilots to "Lost" fast enough**
- If they say no on Day 15, accept it. Log why. Move on. Don't re-pitch 2 weeks later.
