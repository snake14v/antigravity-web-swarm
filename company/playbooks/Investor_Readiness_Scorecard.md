# Investor Readiness Scorecard — Ooru Logix

From Claude analysis: "You do not need VC money yet. You need 5 paying customers and proof of repeatable sales." This document defines exactly WHEN you're ready to fundraise and HOW to approach investors.

**Key principle:** Do NOT approach a single investor until you've achieved the minimum requirements. Investors talk to each other. One bad pitch before you're ready kills your credibility for 6-12 months.

---

## The "Raise-Ready" Checklist

### Absolute Must-Haves (BLOCKING)

You MUST have ALL of these before ANY investor conversation:

#### 1. Minimum Customer Traction
- [ ] **3+ paying ShopSense customers** (not pilots, not friends, PAID)
  - Evidence: Signed invoices with payment received
  - Timeline to achieve: 2-3 months at current sales pace
  - Why: Proves business model works, not just hypothesis

- [ ] **3-month customer data** (each customer in the system ≥12 weeks)
  - Evidence: Monthly P&L showing recurring revenue
  - Timeline: Month 3 onward
  - Why: Investors want to see repeatable unit economics

- [ ] **Customer testimonials with ROI numbers**
  - Format: 2-3 sentence quote per customer with specific ₹ savings
  - Example: "We recovered ₹1,500/week using ShopSense. Paid for itself in 4 months." — Rajesh, Fresh Mart, Bangalore
  - Evidence: Video testimonials or written signed quotes
  - Why: Most powerful sales tool + investor proof

#### 2. Unit Economics Nailed Down
- [ ] **Actual COGS per unit documented**
  - Hardware: Pis, cameras, touchscreen, power = ₹12,500 (assumption)
  - Actual: _____ (must be within 15% of assumption)
  - Evidence: Invoice + receipt folder
  - Why: Proves margins are real, not fantasy

- [ ] **Installation time validated** (actual hours, not estimate)
  - Assumption: 6 hours per installation
  - Actual: _____ hours (from 3+ installs)
  - Evidence: Time logs or daily notes
  - Why: Impacts scalability + your workload

- [ ] **Pilot-to-paid conversion rate documented**
  - Assumption: 60%+ of pilots convert to paid
  - Actual: ___% (from all pilots run)
  - Evidence: Pipeline spreadsheet
  - Why: Unit economics depend on this

- [ ] **Support time per unit per month**
  - Assumption: 2 hours support/month
  - Actual: _____ hours (from deployed units)
  - Evidence: Support ticket log or notes
  - Why: Impacts when you need to hire

#### 3. Financial Proof
- [ ] **Monthly revenue for 2+ consecutive months** (even ₹25K/month)
  - Evidence: Bank statement + invoice log
  - Why: Shows repeatability, not one-off luck

- [ ] **P&L projection for next 12 months**
  - Format: Simple spreadsheet
  - Revenue assumptions clear: # customers × ₹25K
  - Expense assumptions: parts, salary (if you pay yourself), ops
  - Bottom line: Break-even timeline
  - Why: Investor needs to see path to profitability

#### 4. Legal & Operational
- [ ] **Udyam registration completed** (for MSME status)
  - Evidence: Udyam certificate
  - Why: Credibility + eligibility for government schemes

- [ ] **Bank account operational** (in business name, not personal)
  - Evidence: Bank statements showing customer payments
  - Why: Professional operations, auditable records

- [ ] **GST registration** (if revenue >₹20L/year in future)
  - Evidence: GST certificate or registration confirmation
  - Timeline: Do this before fundraising talks
  - Why: Investors will ask, shows you're compliant

- [ ] **Simple cap table** (who owns what)
  - Format: Google Sheet with name, equity %, date
  - Your own entry: 100% (initially)
  - Why: Clarity for investor term sheet

#### 5. Marketing & Visibility
- [ ] **GitHub repo live** with ShopSense open-source components
  - Evidence: github.com/[yourhandle]/shopsense
  - Stars/forks: 10+ is good signal
  - Documentation: README, setup guide, model training notes
  - Why: Founder credibility + engineering rigor

- [ ] **LinkedIn presence**
  - Followers: 200+
  - Posts: 10+ (about ShopSense, learnings, product updates)
  - Engagement: Regular (not ghost account)
  - Why: Founder visibility, investor research you

- [ ] **Demo video** (2-3 minutes, real deployment)
  - Content: [Install] → [Live detection] → [Daily report] → [Customer testimonial]
  - Not promotional, just functional
  - Evidence: YouTube link or Vimeo
  - Why: Investors understand product in 3 min

#### 6. Intellectual Property (if relevant)
- [ ] **YOLO model training data documented**
  - Evidence: Dataset size, accuracy metrics, retrain process
  - Why: IP is valuable, shows technical depth

### Nice-to-Have (Accelerators, not blockers)

These help, but aren't required:

- [ ] **10+ deployed units** (vs 3 minimum)
- [ ] **2 cities** deployed (Bangalore + another city like Chennai, Hyderabad)
- [ ] **Model accuracy documented** on real-world data (80%+ = strong)
- [ ] **Indic language UI** (Hindi, Kannada options)
- [ ] **6-month revenue run rate visible** (shows growth trajectory)
- [ ] **Industry partnership** (e.g., wholesaler, distributor interested)
- [ ] **Press coverage** (even small local outlet)
- [ ] **Award or recognition** (startup awards, hackathon, etc.)

## The 30-Second Pitch

**Keep this short and proof-driven. Adjust when you have real numbers:**

**Version 1 (Before first 3 customers):**
"India has 12 million small retail shops. They lose 15-20% daily revenue to billing errors. ShopSense is two Raspberry Pis and cameras that watch the counter and bill automatically using computer vision. ₹25K one-time, no subscription, no internet needed. We're targeting Bangalore first, piloting with shop owners now."

**Version 2 (After 3 customers, Month 4):**
"India has 12 million small shops losing ₹300+/day to billing errors. ShopSense watches their counter and bills automatically using local computer vision. No internet, no subscription, ₹25K one-time. We have 3 paying customers in Bangalore who've recovered ₹1,500/week each. We're raising ₹50L to scale to 50 units in 6 months."

**Version 3 (After 10 units, Month 6):**
"India has 12 million small shops. We built ShopSense — two-camera, two-Pi system that eliminates billing errors using computer vision. 10 live units, ₹25K price point, 92%+ accuracy. 3-month payback. We're raising ₹50L to hit 50 units by Dec and prove the model for Series A."

**Core elements:**
- Market size (12M shops)
- Problem (₹300/day loss)
- Solution (ShopSense, X₹ price)
- Proof (X customers, ₹Y saved)
- Ask (₹Z for what milestones)

## Term Sheet Expectations

**When you're ready to raise, expect:**

### Amount & Terms
- Amount: ₹50L (₹5 crore / $600K USD)
- Valuation: ₹5 crore pre-money (10% equity = ₹50L)
- Structure: SAFE or convertible note (avoid straight equity for seed)

**Why these numbers:**
- ₹50L runway: 18 months (₹25L for inventory, ₹15L salary, ₹10L ops)
- ₹5Cr valuation: Fair for 3+ paying customers in a ₹50K crore market

### Use of Funds
- 50%: Inventory & COGS (build 50 units = ₹62.5L COGS)
- 30%: Hiring (1 BD + 1 support hire = ₹45L/year)
- 20%: R&D & ops (model training, marketing, infra)

### Expected Milestones (Investor will ask)
- Year 1: 50 units deployed, ₹3-5L MRR, proven unit economics
- Year 2: 200 units, ₹15L MRR, 2-3 cities, first Series A conversations
- Year 3: 1,000+ units, potential acqui-hire by larger retailtech player OR Series A raise

## What Investors Will Ask

Prepare answers for these (they'll ask in coffee chat):

**1. "What's your unfair advantage?"**
Your answer: "I'm the founder using the product daily in Bangalore shops. No one else has spent 1,000 hours understanding the actual pain. Plus, our YOLO model is retrained on real shop data — competitors building elsewhere don't have this."

**2. "Why can't a larger company crush you?"**
Your answer: "Larger retailers (1M+) have different economics. They want subscription SaaS. Our customers are mom-and-pop shops paying ₹25K one-time. Large companies skip this segment. By the time they notice, we'll have 200+ units and switching costs will be high."

**3. "What's the LTV/CAC?"**
Your answer: "LTV: ₹25K (one-time) + ₹500/mo support (assuming 2 years) = ₹37K. CAC: ₹1,500 (time to sell + demo) spread over installers. LTV/CAC = 25x. Healthy."

**4. "Why is this now and not 5 years ago?"**
Your answer: "Computer vision accuracy hit 90%+ only in last 2 years (YOLOv8). Edge TPUs became cheap (Pis are ₹6.5K now vs ₹15K in 2019). Indian small shops now have electricity + WiFi standard. Everything aligned 2024-2025."

**5. "How do you scale without hiring?"**
Your answer: "Year 1: Solo. I focus on 50 units in Bangalore (I can support them remotely + monthly visits). Year 2: Hire 1 support person at ₹30K/mo. By then, revenue covers. By 50 units, my time is 70% sales, 30% engineering. At 100 units, I hire BD person."

**6. "What's your competitive advantage in model accuracy?"**
Your answer: "We retrain monthly on real shop data from deployed units. Competitors train once on public datasets. Our model gets better every month as we learn what items look like in different Indian shop lighting. By 50 units, we'll be 95%+ accurate on items in our deployment footprint."

## Red Flags to Avoid (Kill Your Fundraise)

**Investor Meetings: Do NOT say these things:**

- [ ] "We're waiting for more customers before talking to investors" (you're ready NOW)
- [ ] "Our unit economics aren't validated yet" (validate FIRST)
- [ ] "We're raising because we need to, not because we know what to do with it" (have a 18-month plan)
- [ ] "We're building for 10 other products simultaneously" (focus on ShopSense)
- [ ] "We don't have paying customers yet but..." (you MUST have customers first)
- [ ] "Our COGS might change" (it's locked in, you've built 10 units)
- [ ] "We'll do Series A after this" (don't mention Series A until Series A time)
- [ ] "Our founder is part-time on this" (you're full-time, period)

## Monthly Readiness Review

**First Sunday of every month, ask yourself:**

1. Do I have 3+ paying customers yet? Y/N
2. Is my unit economics locked in (COGS, install time, support time)? Y/N
3. Do I have 2+ months of revenue? Y/N
4. Is my LinkedIn/GitHub/demo video at investor quality? Y/N
5. Can I close 5 customer conversations with the pitch? Y/N (practice with non-investors first)

**If all are YES:** Start warm intros to angels in Month 4-5.

**If any are NO:** Keep building. Fundraising can wait. Customers come first.
